<p><p>Hydrogel Drug Delivery Systems (DDS) are innovative platforms that utilize hydrophilic polymer networks to deliver therapeutic agents effectively. These systems can be categorized into two main types: Physical Crosslinked Hydrogels, which rely on non-covalent interactions like hydrogen bonds and ionic bonds for stability, and Crosslinking Agent Crosslinked Hydrogels, which employ chemical crosslinkers to form strong covalent bonds. Both types offer controlled drug release, enhanced stability, and improved bioavailability, making them pivotal in advancing targeted therapy in pharmaceuticals.</p></p>
