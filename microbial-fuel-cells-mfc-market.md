<p><p>Microbial Fuel Cells (MFC) harness the metabolic processes of microorganisms to convert organic matter into electricity. There are two primary market types: Mediator Microbial Fuel Cells utilize mediators, chemical compounds that enhance electron transfer between microbes and electrodes, improving efficiency. In contrast, Mediator-free Microbial Fuel Cells operate without these compounds, relying solely on natural electron transfer mechanisms. This distinction influences their application in wastewater treatment, energy production, and environmental monitoring, with each type offering unique advantages in efficiency and sustainability.</p></p>
