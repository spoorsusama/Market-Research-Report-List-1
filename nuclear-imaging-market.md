<p><ul><li>Positron Emission Tomography</li><li>Single-photon Emission Computed Tomography</li><li>Planar Scintigraphy Systems</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The nuclear imaging market encompasses various technologies for visualizing internal body processes. Positron Emission Tomography (PET) provides detailed 3D images using radioactive tracers, primarily for oncology, cardiology, and neurology. Single-Photon Emission Computed Tomography (SPECT) offers functional imaging with gamma-ray emitting tracers, useful for diagnosing conditions such as heart disease and brain disorders. Planar Scintigraphy Systems deliver 2D images, commonly used for bone scans and assessing organ function. Each type plays a crucial role in diagnostic and therapeutic applications.</p></p>
