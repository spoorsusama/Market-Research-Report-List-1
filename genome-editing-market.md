<p><p>The genome editing market encompasses various technologies, primarily CRISPR, TALEN, and ZFN. CRISPR (Clustered Regularly Interspaced Short Palindromic Repeats) offers cost-effective and precise editing, making it the most widely used method. TALEN (Transcription Activator-Like Effector Nucleases) provides higher specificity in targeted gene modifications, while ZFN (Zinc Finger Nucleases) employs engineered zinc finger proteins for editing, though it's less common due to its complexity. Each type caters to specific research, therapeutics, and agricultural applications, driving growth in the market.</p></p>
