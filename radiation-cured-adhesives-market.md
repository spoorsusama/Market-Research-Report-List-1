<p><p>Radiation cured adhesives are a type of adhesive that undergoes polymerization through exposure to radiation, typically ultraviolet (UV) light or electron beams. This growth is driven by increasing demand for environmentally friendly products, as radiation curing eliminates the need for solvents, thus reducing volatile organic compound (VOC) emissions. Additionally, advancements in technology are enabling more effective and efficient curing processes, expanding the market's potential applications.</p><p>Latest trends include the rising adoption of UV curable adhesives in the electronics sector, fueled by the miniaturization of electronic components requiring high precision and fast curing times.
