<p><p>Position detection sensors are categorized into two main types: contact and non-contact. Contact sensors require physical interaction with the object to detect its position, often using mechanical or electrical methods, and are typically used in applications where precision is essential. Non-contact sensors, on the other hand, detect position without physical contact, employing technologies such as infrared, ultrasonic, and capacitive methods, making them ideal for applications needing higher durability and minimal wear and tear.</p></p>
